Eric Andersson

I am a research scientist at the American Museum of Natural History. My primary research interest is how the formation of stars and star clusters affect the evolution of galaxies. In my work I develop and test different models of single stars formation, evolution, feedback and enrichment.

INFERNO

INFERNO implements star-by-star calculations to galaxy simulations with the purpose of studying how individual stars affect the baryon cycle in galaxies. The model include, e.g., the effects of runaway stars, chemical enrichment from specific stellar evolutionary stages, and comparisons with resolved-star observations.

Engineering Dwarfs at Galaxy Formation's Edge (EDGE)

The EDGE project investigates the formation of the smallest galaxies in the Universe to learn about the nature of dark matter, the interstellar medium, and star formation in dwarf galaxies. My latest contribution extends the physics model in EDGE with the INFERNO model.

Torch

Torch is a code base that couple magnetohydrodynamics and direct N-body stellar dynamics to simulate the formation of star clusters. Torch makes use of the Astrophysical Multipurpose Software Environment to combine different state-of-the-art software optimized to treat specific physical processes.
